Frequently Asked Questions

What is syllogism in reasoning?
Syllogism is a form of logical reasoning where a conclusion is drawn from two given or assumed propositions (premises). It involves statements like 'All', 'Some', 'No', etc.
How many types of syllogism questions are there?
Syllogism questions can be of various types including Basic Syllogism, Only a Few, Possibility Cases, Reverse Syllogism, and Coded Syllogism.
What is the best approach to solve syllogism questions?
The best approach is to use Venn diagrams to visualize the relationships between different sets. Practice with different types of statements to build confidence.
What is reverse syllogism?
Reverse syllogism is a type where the conclusion is given and you have to find the correct statements that lead to that conclusion. It requires understanding of all types of syllogism statements.
How many questions on syllogism appear in exams?
In most competitive exams like SSC, Banking, and Railway, you can expect 3-5 questions on syllogism. It's a high-scoring topic if practiced well.
