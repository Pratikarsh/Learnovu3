Frequently Asked Questions

What are the basic formulas for train problems?
The basic formulas are: Speed = Distance / Time, Distance = Speed × Time, Time = Distance / Speed. For trains crossing a pole, Distance = Length of train. For crossing a platform, Distance = Length of train + Length of platform.
What is relative speed in train problems?
Relative speed is the speed of one object with respect to another. For trains moving in opposite directions, relative speed = Sum of speeds. For trains moving in the same direction, relative speed = Difference of speeds.
How to solve train crossing a platform problems?
When a train crosses a platform, the total distance covered = Length of train + Length of platform. Use the formula: Time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed of train.
How many questions on train problems appear in exams?
In most competitive exams like SSC, Banking, and Railway, you can expect 2-4 questions on train-based problems. These are high-scoring if practiced well.
